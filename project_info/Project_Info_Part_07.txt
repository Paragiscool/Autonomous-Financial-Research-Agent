SECTION B4: DETAILED SIMULATION MECHANICS 
B4.1 The Research Request Workflow 
How The Simulation Operates: 
In the simulation, research requests arrive through a simulated queue. Each request contains: (1) the 
research question, (2) the requesting stakeholder (portfolio manager, risk officer, or client relationship 
manager), (3) the urgency level (standard: 4-hour SLA, urgent: 1-hour SLA, critical: 30-minute SLA), 
and (4) the expected output format (full report, executive brief, data table, or risk alert). Your agent 
must handle all request types and adjust its research depth based on urgency and output format 
requirements. 
The simulation also models real-world constraints that your agent must handle: API rate limits that 
throttle during peak hours, intermittent network failures on 10% of external API calls, data staleness 
where cached results may be up to 24 hours old, and concurrent requests where the agent may receive 
a new request while still processing a previous one. 
 
B4.2 The Competitive Benchmark 
Human Analyst Baseline: 
Your agent's outputs are compared against a set of benchmark reports written by experienced human 
analysts. The benchmark set includes: (1) a 12-page company profile of Microsoft written by a 
Goldman Sachs associate, (2) an 8-page earnings analysis of Apple written by a Morgan Stanley 
equity research analyst, (3) a 15-page sector comparison of cloud providers written by a Gartner 
senior analyst. These benchmark reports have been scored by the Research Quality Board and 
represent the target quality level your agent should approach. 
 
Scoring Methodology: 
Agent outputs are compared to benchmarks using a combination of automated metrics (factual 
accuracy, citation density, coverage breadth) and LLM-as-judge evaluation (analytical depth, narrative 
quality, actionable insights). The LLM judge uses a detailed scoring rubric with specific criteria for 
each point on a 1-5 scale, reducing scoring subjectivity.
