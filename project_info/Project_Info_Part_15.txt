ROE (Return on Equity): Net Income divided by Shareholders' Equity. Measures how effectively a 
company uses shareholder capital to generate profit. A key metric for comparing profitability across 
companies. 
 
DCF (Discounted Cash Flow): A valuation method that estimates the value of an investment based 
on its expected future cash flows, discounted back to present value using a discount rate (typically 
WACC). The standard method for intrinsic valuation of companies. 
 
Market Capitalization: The total market value of a company's outstanding shares. Calculated as 
Share Price multiplied by Total Shares Outstanding. Used to classify companies by size (mega-cap, 
large-cap, mid-cap, small-cap). 
 
Basis Points (bps): One hundredth of one percentage point (0.01%). Used in finance to describe 
small changes in interest rates, yields, or margins. 100 basis points = 1 percentage point. 
 
SEBI: Securities and Exchange Board of India. The regulatory authority for securities markets in 
India, equivalent to the SEC in the United States. Governs stock exchanges (BSE, NSE), mutual 
funds, and market intermediaries. 
 
EDGAR: Electronic Data Gathering, Analysis, and Retrieval. The SEC's system for companies to 
submit required filings electronically and for the public to access them. The primary data source for 
US regulatory filings. 
 
League Tables: Rankings of investment banks by their market share in specific transaction types 
(M&A advisory, equity underwriting, debt underwriting). Published by providers like Dealogic and 
Refinitiv. Used in Challenge 4 for competitive analysis.
